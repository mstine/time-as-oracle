THE FOOL
The ordinary world
What am I missing?
I'd pull a few, journal them, note what I was feeling, close the book. That practice is real. But one day I'm looking at a hard autumn on a single page, and there it is again and again — the Five of Pentacles. The figure in the snow, outside the lit window. Of seventy-eight cards, the one that wouldn't leave was the one my body already read as lack — in the exact stretch the financial floor was giving way under me. Maybe the count was chance. The match wasn't.
The reframe
A single readingis a sentence. Complete. True.
→
A year of readingsis a novel. One your own cards have been writing.
Most of us have read sentences our whole practice without ever opening the book.

DEATH
What has to die
The habit of treating each reading
as an isolated ritual.
Not your practice — your practice is beautiful. The isolation in which you've been practicing. Pull, interpret, close the circle, each reading its own working. That's real. But it's incomplete.
You already live by cycles
One moon phase isn't
the lunar cycle.
You track the Wheel of the Year. You know Samhain is not Beltane, that October's energy is not April's. You just haven't applied that cyclical awareness to your cards yet.
The caterpillar doesn't become a butterfly.
It becomes goo first.
February 2024.
I pulled Death on the 12th — my rarest card, ten pulls in two years. My sister Julie died on the 18th. Three deaths, three days apart.

THE STAR
And then — something. Far off in the black sky, a shimmer.
A year of your cards, across time
664 readings · 1,178 pulls · 28 months tracked
Wands Cups Swords Pentacles
She had a project.
My most-pulled card that year, clustering in March and again in November. When I looked at what those windows shared, it was obvious — she had a project I was too close to see day by day. That's the descriptive read, and it's honest. But I'm a systems person — so I had to ask whether she was really beating chance.
“
An oracle cannot predict a precise event. But it can cast an image of a certain qualitative field of events — a certain psychological probability.
— Marie-Louise von Franz · On Divination and Synchronicity
So I asked von Franz's two questions
Not the card. The field.
The precise card? Chance. No card beats the odds — even the Five of Pentacles that haunted me: my deck clusters some card like that a third of the time.

The qualitative field? Real. The element keeps time with the moment — what I ask and what I live, both. Earth · money   Fire · making   Water · loss   Air + Water · the Morrigan.

The deck didn't pick the card. It kept time with the charge.
